5 principles that separate good from great.

PRINCIPLE ONE

Start Before You're Ready

Waiting for the perfect camera, the perfect script, the perfect moment? It doesn't exist. Start with what you have. Improve as you go.

PRINCIPLE TWO

Embrace Imperfection

Your first 50 films won't be masterpieces. That's the point. Every imperfect project teaches you something a textbook never could.

3/7

PRINCIPLE THREE

Learn From Every Take

The best filmmakers watch their own work with brutal honesty. Not to punish themselves — but to find the one thing to improve next time.

4/7

PRINCIPLE FOUR

Consistency Over Inspiration

Inspiration is a bonus, not a prerequisite. Show up. Shoot. Learn. Repeat. The habit matters more than the mood.

5/7
05
PRINCIPLE FIVE

Share Your Work

The filmmaker who shares a rough cut learns 10x faster than the one who perfects in private. Put it out there. Get feedback. Grow.
